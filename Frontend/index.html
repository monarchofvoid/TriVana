How NASA Measures Light Curves to Discover Distant Worlds

Light Curve Graph

How It Works:

Transit Method: When an exoplanet passes in front of its host star from our perspective, it blocks a small fraction of the star's light, causing a temporary dip in brightness.

Light Curve: By continuously measuring the star's brightness over time, we can detect these periodic dips and infer the presence, size, and orbital period of the planet.

The Dip: The depth of the brightness dip tells us about the planet's size relative to the star - larger planets create deeper dips.

NASA's Kepler and TESS: Space telescopes like Kepler and TESS have used this method to discover thousands of exoplanets by monitoring the brightness of stars with incredible precision.
